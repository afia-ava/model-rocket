# Model Rocket Project Journal 

## Journal 1 
I mainly started off by designing the rocket body in Autodesk Fusion 360. The main goal was to keep it as lightweight as possible while having enough structural strength. I printed my first design which horribly failed as the engine holding space wasn't enough, was not firmly connected to the rest of the longer rocket body, and the fins were too small to actually be able to launch it. 

## Journal 2
The v2 version changes I worked on included increasing the bottom fin breath more than the height- about 90 mm. I also had to reduce some extra unnecessary things to find the right balanced weight. I fixed the engine case box to the rocket body firmly with another layer of case in between. 

## Journal 3
The v2 version was mostly alright except for the base. In the base part, the engine case had to go down so that after printed I had enough space for the cap to spin down that case tube. I made more changes on this and the final printed version was ready to go! 

## Journal 4
After printing all the 3 parts I had to sand down the connecting parts for a better fit. I installed the engine in the case in the bottom part. I added a parachute in between the top and middle part with a stretchy string. It was secured in the top part with a strong knot and in the middle part with a layer of paper and hot glue. 

## Journal 5
To prepare for the launch, the rest parts were adding wadding papers in the middle tube. I also glued down a straw in the bottom base so the rocket launcher could hold it. Then the rocket was placed on the launch rod, and with power the engine generated thrust leading the rocket to launch. 

## Journal 6
The launch was successful. The parachute deployed in time, and slowed the rocket to come down gradually. I launched it on May 19!
